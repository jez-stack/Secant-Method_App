📚 Mathematical Discussion

The Secant Method is a root-finding algorithm that uses a succession of roots of secant lines to better approximate a root of a function $f(x)$. It requires two initial approximations $x_0$ and $x_1$, and does not need the derivative $f'(x)$ (unlike Newton's method).

Iteration Formula:
$$ x_{n+1} = x_n - f(x_n)\frac{x_n - x_{n-1}}{f(x_n) - f(x_{n-1})} $$

Key Properties:

  • Convergence: Superlinear with order approximately $\phi \approx 1.618$ (the golden ratio)
  • Requirements: Two initial points, function must be continuous
  • Advantage: No derivative needed (unlike Newton's method)
  • Failure: Fails if $f(x_n) \approx f(x_{n-1})$ (division by near-zero)
